About ZZ Plant

The ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia) is a tropical perennial native to eastern Africa, from Kenya to South Africa. It has gained immense popularity as a houseplant due to its extremely low maintenance requirements and ability to survive in conditions that would kill most other plants.

The plant features distinctive, glossy leaves that grow in a feather-like pattern along stems that emerge from a thick, potato-like rhizome underground. This rhizome stores water, allowing the plant to survive long periods without watering.

The ZZ Plant grows slowly but steadily, eventually reaching heights of 2-3 feet indoors. Its architectural form and deep green foliage make it an attractive addition to modern interiors.

Benefits:
  • Extremely drought-tolerant - can go months without water if necessary
  • Thrives in low light conditions, even in windowless rooms with only fluorescent lighting
  • Air-purifying qualities help remove toxins from indoor environments
  • Resistant to pests and diseases
  • Perfect for offices, dorm rooms, and homes with minimal natural light

ZZ Plant Care Guide

Light

Tolerates low light to bright indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight which can scorch the leaves. Can even grow under fluorescent lights.

Water

Allow soil to dry completely between waterings. Water sparingly - once every 2-3 weeks in growing season, less in winter. Overwatering is the main cause of problems.

Temperature

Prefers temperatures between 65-85°F (18-29°C). Can tolerate occasional temperature drops but keep above 45°F (7°C).

Humidity

Adapts to normal household humidity levels. No special humidity requirements needed.

Fertilizer

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half strength once every 3 months during growing season. No fertilizer needed in winter.

Pruning

Minimal pruning required. Remove yellowed or damaged leaves at the base. Can be divided when pot-bound.

Important Note: All parts of the ZZ Plant contain calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause skin irritation. Wash hands after handling and keep away from pets and children who might chew on the leaves.
