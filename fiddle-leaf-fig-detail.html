About Fiddle Leaf Fig

The Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata) is a species of flowering plant in the mulberry and fig family Moraceae. It is native to western Africa, from Cameroon west to Sierra Leone, where it grows in lowland tropical rainforest.

As a houseplant, it's prized for its large, glossy, violin-shaped leaves that can grow up to 18 inches long. The plant can reach heights of 6 feet or more indoors, making it an impressive statement piece in any room.

The Fiddle Leaf Fig became incredibly popular in interior design during the 2010s and continues to be one of the most sought-after houseplants due to its dramatic appearance and ability to serve as a focal point in home decor.

Benefits:
  • Creates a bold, dramatic focal point in any room
  • Purifies indoor air by removing toxins
  • Adds a touch of nature and tropical feel to interior spaces
  • Can grow quite tall, filling empty vertical space in rooms with high ceilings
  • Relatively low maintenance once acclimated to its environment

Fiddle Leaf Fig Care Guide

Light

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Can tolerate some direct morning sun, but avoid harsh afternoon sunlight which can scorch the leaves. Rotate periodically for even growth.

Water

Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil are dry. Typically once every 7-10 days, but adjust based on your home's conditions. Ensure pot has good drainage and avoid overwatering.

Temperature

Prefers temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Avoid cold drafts, sudden temperature changes, and temperatures below 55°F (13°C).

Humidity

Enjoys moderate to high humidity (40-60%). Consider using a humidifier or pebble tray with water in dry environments.

Fertilizer

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half strength once a month during growing season (spring and summer). No fertilizer needed in winter.

Pruning

Prune in spring or summer to control size and shape. Remove damaged or yellowing leaves at the base. Wear gloves as the sap can cause skin irritation.

Pro Tip: Fiddle Leaf Figs don't like to be moved frequently. Find a good spot with appropriate light and try to keep it there. They may drop leaves when stressed by environmental changes.
