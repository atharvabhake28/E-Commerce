About Peace Lily

The Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum) is a popular flowering houseplant native to tropical regions of the Americas and southeastern Asia. It features elegant white "flowers" (actually modified leaves called spathes) and glossy, dark green foliage.

Peace Lilies are known for their air-purifying abilities and were included in NASA's Clean Air Study for their effectiveness in removing indoor pollutants like benzene, formaldehyde, and trichloroethylene.

These plants are also known for being excellent communicators - they visibly droop when they need water, making them perfect for beginners who are still learning plant care.

Benefits:
  • Excellent air purifier, removing common household toxins
  • Thrives in low light conditions, perfect for darker corners of your home
  • Produces beautiful white flowers throughout the year
  • Clearly shows when it needs water by drooping its leaves
  • Relatively low maintenance and forgiving for beginners

Peace Lily Care Guide

Light

Thrives in low to medium indirect light. Can tolerate lower light conditions, but will produce fewer flowers. Avoid direct sunlight which can scorch the leaves.

Water

Water when the top inch of soil is dry or when leaves begin to droop slightly. Use room temperature water. Peace Lilies are sensitive to chemicals in tap water, so filtered water is preferable.

Temperature

Prefers temperatures between 65-85°F (18-29°C). Keep away from cold drafts and sudden temperature changes.

Humidity

Enjoys higher humidity levels (50-60%). Regular misting, a humidifier, or placing on a pebble tray with water can help maintain humidity.

Fertilizer

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half strength every 6-8 weeks during growing season (spring and summer). Reduce or eliminate fertilizing in fall and winter.

Pruning

Remove spent flowers by cutting them off at the base. Trim yellowing or damaged leaves at the base of the stem. Clean scissors or pruning shears are recommended.

Important Note: Peace Lilies are toxic to cats, dogs, and humans if ingested. Keep out of reach of pets and children. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals which can cause irritation and swelling if consumed.
