About Pothos

The Pothos (Epipremnum aureum), also known as Devil's Ivy, is a versatile and popular houseplant native to Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves that come in various shades of green, often with variegation patterns of white, yellow, or cream, depending on the variety.

Pothos is known for its trailing vines that can grow several feet long, making it perfect for hanging baskets, shelves, or training to climb up a moss pole or trellis. In its natural habitat, it can grow up to 40 feet, with leaves reaching up to 30 inches in length.

This plant has earned its nickname "Devil's Ivy" because it's nearly impossible to kill and stays green even when kept in the dark. It's one of the most adaptable houseplants available, thriving in a wide range of conditions.

Benefits:
  • Excellent air purifier, removing toxins like formaldehyde, benzene, and carbon monoxide
  • Extremely adaptable to various light conditions, from low light to bright indirect light
  • Drought-tolerant and forgiving if you forget to water
  • Fast-growing, providing visible results and satisfaction
  • Available in multiple varieties with different leaf patterns and colors
  • Perfect for beginners or those who travel frequently

Pothos Care Guide

Light

Adaptable to various light conditions, from low light to bright indirect light. More variegated varieties need brighter light to maintain their patterns. Avoid direct sunlight which can scorch the leaves.

Water

Allow soil to dry out between waterings. Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil feel dry to the touch. Reduce watering in winter. Pothos is more tolerant of underwatering than overwatering.

Humidity

Tolerates normal household humidity levels. Higher humidity will encourage larger leaves and faster growth, but it's not necessary for the plant's survival.

Fertilizer

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half strength once every 2-3 months during growing season (spring and summer). No fertilizer needed in fall and winter.

Pruning & Propagation

Prune as needed to control length and encourage bushier growth. Pothos is extremely easy to propagate - simply cut a stem with at least one node, remove lower leaves, and place in water until roots develop (usually 2-3 weeks).

Pro Tip: To keep your Pothos looking full and bushy, periodically trim the vines and replant the cuttings back into the same pot after rooting them in water.
Note: Pothos is toxic to pets if ingested. Keep out of reach of cats, dogs, and small children.
