About Snake Plant

The Snake Plant (Sansevieria trifasciata) is a popular houseplant known for its striking, sword-like leaves that grow upright. Native to West Africa, this resilient plant has adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it one of the most forgiving houseplants available.

Its distinctive appearance features stiff, upright leaves with green banding patterns and yellow borders on some varieties. The plant can grow anywhere from 8 inches to several feet tall, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

Beyond its aesthetic appeal, the Snake Plant is renowned for its air-purifying abilities. NASA's Clean Air Study found that it's one of the best plants for removing toxins like formaldehyde, xylene, and toluene from the air.

Benefits:
  • Excellent air purifier, removing toxins and releasing oxygen at night
  • Extremely drought-tolerant and can survive with minimal watering
  • Thrives in low light conditions, making it perfect for darker corners
  • Resistant to most pests and diseases
  • Perfect for beginners or those who travel frequently

Snake Plant Care Guide

Light

Tolerates low light but thrives in indirect bright light. Can tolerate some direct sun, but avoid harsh afternoon sunlight.

Water

Allow soil to dry completely between waterings. Water sparingly, especially in winter. Overwatering is the most common cause of problems.

Temperature

Prefers temperatures between 60-85°F (15-29°C). Can tolerate occasional temperature drops but protect from frost.

Humidity

Adapts to normal household humidity levels. No special humidity requirements needed.

Fertilizer

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half strength once every 2-3 months during growing season (spring and summer).

Pruning

Minimal pruning required. Remove damaged leaves by cutting them off at the base. Can be divided when pot-bound.

Pro Tip: Snake plants prefer to be slightly pot-bound, so don't rush to repot them. When you do repot, choose a container just slightly larger than the current one.
